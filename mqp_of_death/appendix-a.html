Appendix A:
Motion Picture Projection at WPI

The WPI Lens and Lights club was formed in 1962 to facilitate the need for motion picture projection on the campus of Worcester Polytechnic Institute (WPI) in Worcester, Massachusetts. Starting with a pair of Simplex Carbon-Arc 35mm projectors, the student-run club projected movies weekly in Alden Hall on campus. In 1989, WPI and the WPI Lens and Lights Club received a pair of Norelco Todd-AO AA-II Xenon-Arc 35mm/70mm motion picture projectors as a gift from the National Amusements Corporation. While the WPI Lens and Lights club has expanded to cover audio and lighting needs of the WPI community and beyond, motion picture projection remains the foundation on which they build.

Currently, the WPI Lens and Lights Club is contracted by the WPI Social Committee to project movies every week in Perreault Hall. A Head Projectionist, who is elected by the Lens and Lights Club, is in charge of all motion picture operations at WPI. The Head Projectionist is in charge of training and sponsoring new projectionists. Once trained, a projectionists in training is sent to the Department of Public Safety to complete his/her projectionist examination. If the examination is passed, s/he becomes licensed and is allowed to work at movie theaters around the state.
